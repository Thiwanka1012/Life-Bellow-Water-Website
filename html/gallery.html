JELLY FISH:

Jellyfish are gelatinous marine creatures with stinging tentacles, belonging to the phylum Cnidaria. They drift with ocean currents, feeding on small fish and plankton.

Their venomous tentacles serve for both defense and prey capture. While crucial to marine ecosystems, their populations are increasing, posing challenges due to factors like overfishing and climate change.

SEA URCHIN:

Sea urchins are marine creatures with spherical bodies covered in sharp spines. They inhabit oceans globally, grazing on algae and organic matter in rocky areas and coral reefs.

With a specialized feeding structure called Aristotle's lantern, they scrape algae from surfaces. Reproducing sexually, they release eggs and sperm into the water. They play crucial roles as grazers in marine ecosystems, but overpopulation can lead to ecosystem degradation.

DOLPHINES:

Dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals known for their playful behavior and sleek bodies. They communicate through vocalizations and form social groups called pods.

Dolphins are carnivores, feeding on fish, squid, and crustaceans. Threats to dolphins include habitat destruction, pollution, and bycatch in fishing gear. Conservation efforts are vital for their protection.

Close

STAR FISH:

Starfish, or sea stars, are marine creatures with radial symmetry and distinctive arms. They come in various sizes and colors, with around 2,000 species known.

Starfish use tube feet for movement and prey capture. They feed by evertng their stomach to digest prey externally. Starfish play a vital role in marine ecosystems as predators and prey. They can regenerate lost limbs, and their unique appearance makes them popular subjects of scientific study and fascination.

Close

OCTOPUS:

The octopus is a highly intelligent marine mollusk with eight arms lined with suction cups. It's known for its camouflage abilities and complex behaviors, including problem-solving.

Octopuses are carnivorous, feeding on various marine organisms. Threats to octopuses include overfishing, habitat destruction, and pollution, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.

Close

SEA TURTLE:

Sea turtles are large marine reptiles known for their streamlined shells and graceful swimming. They feed on seagrasses, algae, and marine organisms.

Sea turtles undertake remarkable migrations and return to nesting beaches to lay eggs. Threats include habitat loss, pollution, and accidental capture in fishing gear, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.

Close

CORAL REEFS:

Coral reefs, built by tiny coral polyps, are vibrant ecosystems supporting diverse marine life. They offer critical services like coastal protection and fisheries support.

Threats such as climate change and pollution endanger their survival. Conservation measures, including marine protection and sustainable practices, are essential for their preservation and the well-being of marine ecosystems and coastal communities.

Close

SEA HORSE:

Seahorses, with their horse-like appearance, inhabit coastal waters worldwide, often among seagrass beds or coral reefs. Their unique reproductive behavior involves males carrying and incubating eggs in a pouch.

Despite their charm, seahorses face threats from habitat destruction, overfishing, and trade. Conservation is vital to safeguard these charismatic creatures and their habitats.
